England 6-2 Wales

England’s world cup campaign got off to a flying start on Monday; firing in six goals against a turgid Iranian side in their opening Group B fixture in Qatar.

Gareth Southgate’s boys produced a masterclass in attacking football, leading 3-0 at the break thanks to sublime finishes from Bellingham, Saka and Sterling. The festival of fine finishing continued in the second half as Saka grabbed another and substitutes Grealish and Rashford piled on the misery as beleaguered Iran manager - and Portugese real estate mogul - Carlos Queiroz saw his side relentlessly pummelled by a dazzling display.

The Iranian’s, oft described as ‘useful’, looked a shadow of the side that had powered itself to 20th in the Fifa Rankings. Never looking likely to trouble Jordan Pickford’s goal throughout, Team Melli can take a slither of consolation from a well taken goal and a (scandalously awarded) penalty from Taremi.

The Three Lions continue their march of destruction through the group stage against the USA on Saturday; Iran renew ancient rivalries against Wales on Friday.
