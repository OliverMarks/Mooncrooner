Tunisia 0-1 Australia

A Mitchell Duke header gave Australia a much needed win over Tunisia as the Socceroos bounced back into life in Group D.

After being soundly beaten by France in their opener, this was seen as a must win game for the Aussies and they started well in the first half, showing much more the attacking threat that was only briefly hinted at against the French. Their reward came in the 23rd minute when Mitchell Dukeâ€™s flicked header saw the ball fly into the far corner of the net, silencing the largely Tunisian crowd.

Tunisia grew into the game in the second half, troubling Mat Ryan in the Australian goal multiple times searching for the equaliser but the Aussies remained resolute in defence, earning their first win of the tournament.

Australia are very much alive in the World Cup again, and a positive result against Denmark in their final game could see them advance to the last 16. For Tunisia, they need nothing less than a win against France in their final game.
