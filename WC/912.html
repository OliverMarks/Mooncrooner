Uruguay 0-0 South Korea

Uruguay and South Korea shared the points in the opening match of Group H at the Education City Stadium.

In a surprisingly entertaining tie for a game in which neither side managed a shot on target - the first time this occurred in a World Cup match in over a century - Uruguay came closest to breaking the deadlock, twice hitting the woodwork. Veteran CB Diego Godin with the first effort from a header in the first half and the effervesant Fede Valverde going close at the end with a thumping strike.

The Taegeuk Warriors threatened throughout but lacked quality in the final third. Hwang Ui-Jo epitomised their issues in front of goal when he flashed over the bar from 6yards out when a goal looked all but certain.

Both sides will still feel very much in the hunt for qualification from Group H as the Sky Blues prepare for their next match against group favourites Portugal as South Korea take on Ghana.
