Denmark 0-0 Tunisia

The points were shared in a 0-0 draw between Denmark and Tunisia inside of a raucous Education City stadium.

Roared on by a feverish crowd, Tunisia wasted no time imposing themselves physically on their Danish opponents, flying into challenges much to the delight of their travelling fans. The Danes failed to get into their rhythm and looked off the pace in the bruising encounter but credit should be given to the Carthage Eagles for not allowing Denmark to settle.

Both sides had goals ruled out by VAR but neither side was able to break the deadlock with Andreas Cornelius having the best chance of the match when he somehow failed to find the net from one yard out.

Potential for late drama arrived in the 95th minute when the referee was asked by his video assistants to review a potential handball by Yassine Merriah inside the Tunisian box. However, Cesar Ramos stuck with his original decision and no penalty was awarded.



The result will give Tunisia hopes of qualification as they face Australia in their next Group D match. The Danish Dynamite will hope to get their tournament rolling against holders France on Saturday.
