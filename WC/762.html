Canada 1-2 Morocco

An at-times chaotic 90 minutes at the Al-Thumama Stadium on Thursday evening ultimately saw Morocco get the better of a plucky Canada outfit 2-1; securing their passage through to the Round of 16 as Group F winners.

Morocco needed just a point to ensure qualification, but were gifted an opening goal after four minutes when goalkeeper Milan Borjan took a heavy touch and allowed Hakim Ziyech to lift into an empty net from 30 yards out.

Midway through the first half, they doubled their lead against a shambolic Canada side, with Youssef En Nesyri running behind the Canadian defence to fire home his first goal of the tournament. Canada halved that deficit five minutes from half time, however, when a cross from the left hand side was turned into his own net by a stretching Nayef Aguerd, taking the record of both the 100th goal of the tournament and the first own goal so far, and handing Canada a potential lifeline back into the game.
