Mexico 0-0 Poland

Poland squandered an opportunity to take control of their qualification from Group C as their opening game against Mexico ended 0-0.

Mexican supporters were out in force in a packed Stadium 974 but the frenzied atmosphere was never quite matched by quality on the pitch by either side. Following a tame first half, the best chance of the game fell to Poland captain Robert Lewandowski when he was brought down in the box and, following an intervention from VAR was awarded a penalty in the second half.

Unfortunately for the Biało-czerwoni Lewandowski’s tame attempt from the spot was well saved by veteran Mexican keeper Guillermo Ochoa. Lewandowski’s miss means the star striker still has not netted at a World Cup.

Although things improved for both sides in the second half, neither team were able to find a clinical edge in the final third. The earlier defeat of Argentina at the hands of Saudi Arabia leaves the group wide open, a situation El Tri will seek to capitalise on against Argentina on Sunday. Poland face surprise group leaders Saudi Arabia.
