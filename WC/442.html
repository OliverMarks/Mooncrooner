Argentina 2-0 Mexico

Lionel Messi inspired Argentina’s revival in Group C, scoring to give his side the lead and get their qualification back on track against Mexico.

After suffering a shock loss to Saudi Arabia, Argentina knew anything but a win would place them in danger of an early exit from this year's tournament. The Albicelestes struggled to get going in a first half heavily punctuated with fouls and stoppages. Just after the hour mark their legendary playmaker made the difference, firing in with his left foot from 20 yards out to beat Mexician keeper Ochoa.

The points were secured when Enzo Fernandez cut in from the left of the box to wondrously curl past Ochoa’s far post. Fernandez became the youngest player to score at a World Cup for Argentina since Messi himself in 2006.

Mexico sit bottom of Group C with a single point and must beat Saudi Arabia to have any chance of qualifying. A win for Argentina in their final match against Poland will ensure qualification.
